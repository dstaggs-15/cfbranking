CFB Top 25

    How this ranking works (plain English)

    This is a results-first, unbiased ranking. It is not seeded by preseason polls or brand names. It uses finished games only from the current season.

    Step 1 — Who’s eligible

    • Only FBS programs are ranked. (No Division II/III or FCS teams.)
    • A team’s record shows every game they played against any opponent if that team is FBS (so 5–1 means five total wins and one total loss you’d recognize on TV).

    Step 2 — Loss buckets

    Teams are grouped by total losses. Fewer losses always come first. A 5–1 team is listed ahead of 4–2 by default.

    Step 3 — Head-to-head inside the bucket

    If two teams in the same loss group played each other, the head-to-head winner is placed ahead of the loser.

    Step 4 — Composite score (used to order within buckets)

    The composite score is a weighted blend of football-common-sense items:

    • Winning percentage (50%): Win the games you play.
    • Strength of schedule (22%): We measure schedule strength using only FBS vs FBS games. It’s the average winning percentage of your FBS opponents.
    • Average scoring margin (10%): Scoring more than you allow matters, but we cap this so blowouts don’t dominate.
    • Quality wins: Beating teams that are roughly Top-40 (based on provisional seeding) adds credit.
    • Bad losses: Losing to roughly 80+ ranked teams deducts credit.
    • Tiny advanced flavor: We add small adjustments based on two season-long efficiency measures:
      • Predicted points added (offense minus defense) — how much each side increases or decreases expected points.
      • Success rate (offense minus defense) — how often plays meet the down-and-distance goal.
      These are small tie-breakers, not the driver.

    Why you won’t see “random” teams up top

    • Schedule strength and bad-loss penalties prevent soft slates from floating to the top.
    • Head-to-head inside the same loss group stops “you beat me but I’m ranked higher” nonsense.

    Translation: Win a lot, against good teams, without bad stumbles, and you’ll rise. Lose more, or play cupcakes, and you won’t.
